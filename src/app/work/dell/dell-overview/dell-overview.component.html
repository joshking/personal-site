This was a great experience with a larger company. I hadn't worked in this type of company before. It was a little intimidating at first, but once I got my feet wet, I fit in just fine.

I worked within the UX team as a user interface developer. It was a great team that did some excellent work.

I wore a few different hats here, but my primary focus was on a question and answer site called itninja.com. It had a loyal user base, and was used worldwide. My main responsibility was the front-end development of the site. I was also able to use my UX skills and put together workflows. I didn't do a lot of visual design, but was often called upon to.

Our team was pretty small on Itninja, so we all had to work together pretty closely. I worked directly with product managers and project managers to always be one sprint ahead of the main development team. I also worked closely with the user testing that was involved, often times putting together functional prototypes for users to destroy.

The other main project was an appliance based service desk application - both a native mobile app and website. It was incredibly complex and fully flexible from the customers standpoint, so there were a lot of variables to consider with even the slightest design change.

I learned a ton while working here. Really grateful for the opportunity.
